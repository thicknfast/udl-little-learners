## Sample 30-Minute Student-Centered Phonics Block

**Grade Level:** Early Childhood / Kindergarten -- 1st Grade

**Focus:** Short vowels (CVC words)

---

### 5 min -- Quick Whole Group Warm-Up

**Purpose:** Activate prior knowledge, introduce skill of the day.

**Activity:**

- 3-5 minute chant or sound warm-up (e.g., CVC words with the short "a" sound: cat, bat, mat).
- Use visuals and gestures to reinforce sounds.
- Teacher observes for participation, noting who is confident vs. who struggles.

---

### 20 min -- Small Group / Station Rotation

**Purpose:** Targeted instruction and practice for all learners at their level.

**Structure:** Students choose or rotate through 2-3 stations (8-10 minutes each) or work independently if small class size. Teacher works with a small group at a time.

#### Station Examples

**Build It Station**
- Students use letter tiles, magnetic letters, or playdough to build CVC words.
- Say each sound aloud as they build.
- Extension: Students write a sentence using one of their words.

**Sound Detectives / Write the Room**
- Students search the classroom or picture cards for words with the target sound.
- Record or draw their findings on a clipboard.
- Extension: Partner discussion to explain why each word matches the sound.

**Listen and Record Station**
- Students read target words aloud into a recording device or with a partner.
- Play back to check accuracy.
- Extension: Students self-assess or mark words they need more practice on.

#### Teacher Small Group Focus

- Work with 4-6 students who need targeted support.
- Activities: guided decoding, sound segmentation, CVC blending.
- Adjust pace and scaffold based on student needs.

---

### 5 min -- Share & Reflection

**Purpose:** Reinforce learning, encourage student voice, check understanding.

**Activity:**

- Quick sharing circle: Each student shows one word or activity from their station.
- Teacher prompts with reflection questions:
  - "Which word was easiest/hardest today?"
  - "How did you figure out a word you didn't know?"
- Highlight student progress and celebrate mastery.
